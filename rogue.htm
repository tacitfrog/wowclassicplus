Primary Goals:
  • Make viable tanking builds that enable a Rogue to tank in 5-man dungeons from level 30; these builds should be centred in Combat and have viable complements in each of the other trees, without spoiling existing builds.
  • Add a tanking poison that does not run out of charges (similar to Crippling Poison). [Spiteful Poison.]
  • Add mechanisms to grab AOE agro (since AOE is intentionally hard with the combo point system). [Blade Dance, Spitebomb.]
  • Make heavy Subtlety builds viable and functionally unique.
  • Add passive bleed synergy to Hemorrhage/Backstab. [Deadliness.]
  • Add passive threat re-direction as a Subtlety mechanic, both to help when tanking and to vent the party when not tanking. [Sadistic Smile.]
  • Give thrown weapons some unique perks to balance out the lack of stats. [Poisons, Deadly Throw.]
Assassination
  • Improved Expose Armor & Murder (swapped positions)
  • Improved Backstab (moved from Combat tree [from TBC])
  • Vile Poisons (also applies to Spitebombs)
  • Crippling Brew (Other poisons and spitebombs also apply Crippling Poison [from WOTLK])
  • Vigor (regains energy, requires 5/5 Seal Fate)
Combat
  • Improved Sprint (available earlier)
  • Combat Acumen (renamed from Improved Slice and Dice; moved from Assassination tree [from TBC]; includes Blade Dance)
  • Endurance (-1 minute per point)
  • Deadened Nerves (passive defence)
  • Dagger Specialization & Fist Weapon Specialization (merged [from WOTLK])
  • Blade Dance (tanking finishing move [from SOD], periodic AOE threat)
  • Blade Flurry (poisons and effects apply)
Subtlety
  • Master of Deception (merged Sleight of Hand)
  • Initiative (available earlier)
  • Elusiveness (-1 minute per point)
  • Sadistic Smile (passive threat transfer)
  • Improved Sap (100% reliable [from TBC]; available earlier for low level dungeons)
  • Heightened Senses (available earlier)
  • Cheat Death ([from TBC]; requires a defensive ability to be active, so not just a freebie; helps mitigate randomness in avoidance tanking)
  • Hemorrhage (110%, stronger bonus but fewer charges [from TBC]; added extra rank)
  • Deadliness (refreshes bleeds, increases damage against bleeding targets)
  • Premeditation (increases damage of next attack on the target [from Shadowstep, TBC], increased duration)
Modified Items
  •  
    Handbook of Eviscerate IX
    The Manual of Eviscerate IX has had its name and icon changed, consistent with other Rogue books.
Modified Abilities
  •  
    Poisons
    Poisons can now be removed from a weapon by using a Silk Towelette. See under Tailoring.

    Wound Poison has been buffed. Wound Poison and Crippling Poison (Rank 2) have had their icons changed [from TBC]. See under Poisons.

    In addition, the poison applied to the offhand weapon now also counts as being applied to any thrown weapon that the Rogue uses. The Throw and Deadly Throw abilities benefit from all poison-enhancing bonuses and talents.

  •  
    Throw
    Throw now toggles autoshoot mode.

    New thrown weapons with on-hit effects are available from Ravenholdt.

New Abilities
  •  
    Spiteful Poison and Spitebombs
    A new poison for tanking (Spite Poison), a new poison grenade (Spitebomb), and the component needed to craft these (Spite Powder) have been added. See under Poisons.
  •  
    Deadly Throw
    Cast: instant. Range: 8 - 30 yards.
    Cost: 35 energy.
    Finishing move that reduces the movement speed of the target by 50% for 1 second plus 1 additional second per combo point and causes damage based on your thrown weapon, attack power, and number of combo points used. (Requires Thrown Weapon.) [From TBC.]

    [Rank 1, learned from a level 60 bind-on-equip handbook dropped by bosses in Karazhan Tower.]
