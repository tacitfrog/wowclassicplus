Coin
  •  
    Coin Trading
    To inhibit game-breaking gold trading, coinboxes have been added.

    Coins can no longer be sent directly by mail. Instead, players who want to send money to other characters must create or buy coinboxes and put the money into them to send it by mail. A coinbox can hold up to 100 gold and can be attached to a mail just like other items. The maximum amount of money that can be sent by a single mail is 1200 gold (12 full coinboxes). The cash-on-delivery interface has been changed to have 12 item slots that can only accommodate coinboxes, so that the player can attach coinboxes containing gold to pay the cash-on-delivery charge (and therefore the maximum charge is 1200 gold).

    Coins can no longer be traded directly between players. Instead, players must first put the gold into coinboxes and can then trade the coinboxes in a trade window. The coin sections at the bottom of the trade window are no longer interactable, but now display the total amount of coin that is currently in the trade window inside coinboxes.

    When the player right-clicks on a coinbox in his inventory, an interface window appears to input the number of gold, silver, and copper coins to be added (by default) or withdrawn (by selecting the appropriate radio box). When a player hovers over a coinbox, the amount of coins within it is displayed in the tooltip text. Coinboxes are not consumed when emptied and the player can freely add or withdraw as much and as often as he wants. Coinboxes can be mailed or traded whether empty or not, can be auctioned when empty, and do not stack.

Mail
  •  
    Coin Trading
    Coins can no longer be sent directly by mail; they must first be put into a coinbox. See under Coin.
  •  
    Enchanted Mail
    Enchanters can now send enchantments by mail. See under Enchanting.
  •  
    Express Mail
    Express Mail is a mailbox service available to all players. By checking the new 'Express Mail' checkbox on a regular mail, it will arrive in the recipient's mailbox within 30 seconds of being sent (rather than the usual 1 hour). The fee for sending Express Mail is 3 silver plus 3 silver per additional attachment (rather than the usual 30 copper). Cash-on-delivery can be used as usual with Express Mail.
Storage
  •  
    Bank Space
    The player bank is now a 7x4 grid, providing a total capacity of 28 spaces (up from 6x4=24) [from TBC]. The bank also offers a seventh purchasable bag slot that can be unlocked at a cost of 250g.
  •  
    Mining Sacks
    Bags for mining materials have been added. See under Leatherworking.
Dual Specs
  •  
    Second Talent Spec
    At level 40, each character gets a second talent spec. The character will then always have one active spec that is currently applied and one inactive spec that currently has no effect.

    Characters can switch between specs by using their Focus Lodestone.

    When a character first reaches level 40, the new spec appears as an empty inactive spec.

    When a character pays to unlearn and relearn talents, only the active spec is modified.

  •  
    Focus Lodestone
    Binds when picked up. Unique.
    A Focus Lodestone can be obtained for free by any character at level 40 or higher by talking to an appropriate class trainer. It can only be used while out of combat and under the effect of Rested (in a friendly/neutral inn or capital city); it has a 10 second cast and a 1 hour cooldown. When used, it switches the character's specs so that the inactive spec becomes active.

    [Note: This item is deliberately similar to a Hearthstone, but for talents.]
