Endgame Raids
  •  
    Deep Delve (Debuff)
    A new debuff called Deep Delve is applied to all players while inside any 40-man PVE raid dungeon (Molten Core, Onyxia's Lair, Desolate Valley, Blackwing Lair, Ahn'Qiraj, and Naxxramas). This debuff represents the gloom and despair of the deepest, darkest places in Azeroth and nullifies the effects of all buffs that are not applied by players or consumables. In other words, World Buffs no longer have any effect in 40-man raid dungeons.

    [Note: This change is meant to retain the quintessentially-Vanilla, over-the-top nature of World Buffs and to preserve them as a fun addition to the game, but to remove the toxicity associated with them and to restore the Vanilla experience to raid content (World Buffs were not mandated by guilds back in the day).]

  •  
    Power Creep Mitigation
    To reduce power creep, many items from Blackwing Lair, Ahn'Qiraj, and Naxxramas, especially weapons and trinkets, have had their item levels reduced and their stats re-balanced accordingly.

    Naxxramas gear has been nerfed the hardest, although it remains the highest item level gear in the game. This is because Naxxramas gear was originally balanced so that it would remain relevant up to level ~65 into TBC, it was not balanced for long-term usage in Vanilla: it is simply overpowered in Vanilla.

    Tiered set pieces have had their primary stats reduced to meet their new item levels. Tier 1 set pieces remain at item level 66. Tier 2/2.5/3 set items have had their item levels reduced from 76/78-88/86-92 to 72/74-78/76-82. These sets retain their powerful set bonuses and prestigious looks. (The Rank 12-13 PVP sets remain at item level 71-74.)

  •  
    Debuff Limit
    The maximum number of debuffs that can be on a target has been increased from 16 to 20.

    Channeled attacks that are simultaneously debuffs, such as Drain Life and Mind Flay, are now treated independently of the debuff limit; they do not count towards it, are not restricted by it, and do not knock off other debuffs if the limit is reached.

    [Note: This change is meant to make a small accommodation for alternative specs without enabling any massive shift in meta, while also providing some space for balance given the introduction of Deep Delve.]

  •  
    Tier 2.5 Acquisition
    The Brood of Nozdormu reputation required to acquire T2.5 set pieces has been increased. To obtain the boots and shoulders, players will need Friendly (up from Neutral). To obtain the helmet and legs, players will need Honoured (up from Friendly). And to obtain the chest, players will need Revered (up from Honoured).

Tier 1.5
  •  
    Tier 1.5 Set
    New 5-piece tier set for each class. These sets have item levels 66-70 and contain the following pieces: head, shoulders, chest, legs, and ring. They cater to specs that are usually not provisioned, such as Rogue tank, Shadow Priest, and Feral Druid. Set bonuses:
    Hunter:
    3: Increases the damage done by your stings by 10%.
    5: Increases the maximum focus of your pets by 20.
    Mage:
    3: All damage you take while channeling Evocation is reduced by 90%.
    5: Critical hits from your Arcane Missiles spell invigorate you for 45-105 health and mana.
    Rogue:
    3: When you dodge, you regain 2 energy (cannot occur more than once per second).
    5: When you suffer a critical hit, the chance you'll be crit by any attack is reduced by 90% for 8 seconds ('Lurk in the Shadows' effect; cannot occur more than once per minute).
    Shaman:
    3: Reduces the mana cost of your shock spells by 5%.
    5: Your critical hits from offensive spells and weapon attacks cause one of your Lightning Shield orbs to strike the target (the usual proc rate limit of Lightning Shield still stands, regardless of whether its triggered the usual way or by this effect).
    Warlock:
    3: Reduces the mana cost of your curses by 10%.
    5: Your Immolate ticks have a 7% chance to make your next Searing Pain spell instant cast ('Firestorm' effect; lasts 10 seconds).
  •  
    Tier 1.5 Acquisition
    T1.5 pieces drop from bosses in Karazhan Tower and from Lord Kazzak:
    • Head: dropped by Prince Malchezaar.
    • Shoulders: dropped by The Curator.
    • Chest: dropped by Lord Kazzak.
    • Legs: dropped by Netherspite.

    Two class-specific pieces drop per boss kill.

    For the ring: class-specific bind-on-pickup gems drop from the Chess and Opera events (1 per event, random classes) in Karazhan Tower. The player will need to collect the class-specific gem, a Nightmare Clasp (see under Blacksmithing), and 3 Mysterious Runes. He will then need to be Friendly with the Violet Eye in order to have the faction craft these materials into the ring for him.
