Endgame
  •  
    Deep Delve (Debuff)
    A new debuff called Deep Delve is applied to all players while inside any 40-man PVE raid dungeon (Molten Core, Onyxia's Lair, Desolate Valley, Blackwing Lair, Ahn'Qiraj, and Naxxramas). This debuff represents the gloom and despair of the deepest, darkest places in Azeroth and nullifies the effects of all buffs that are not applied by players or consumables. In other words, World Buffs no longer have any effect in 40-man raid dungeons.

    [Note: This change is meant to retain the quintessentially-Vanilla, over-the-top nature of World Buffs and to preserve them as a fun addition to the game, but to remove the toxicity associated with them and to restore the Vanilla experience to raid content (World Buffs were not mandated by guilds back in the day).]

  •  
    Power Creep Mitigation
    To reduce power creep, many items from Blackwing Lair, Ahn'Qiraj, and Naxxramas, especially weapons and trinkets, have had their item levels reduced and their stats re-balanced accordingly.

    Naxxramas gear has been nerfed the hardest, although it remains the highest item level gear in the game. This is because Naxxramas gear was originally balanced so that it would remain relevant up to level ~67 into TBC, it was not balanced for long-term usage in Vanilla: it is simply overpowered at level 60.

    Tiered set pieces have had their primary stats reduced to meet their new item levels. Tier 1 set pieces remain at item level 66. Tier 2/2.5/3 set items have had their item levels reduced from 76/78-88/86-92 to 72/74-78/76-82. These sets retain their powerful set bonuses and prestigious looks. (The Rank 12-13 PVP sets remain at item level 71-74.)

  •  
    Debuff Limit
    The maximum number of debuffs that can be on a target has been increased from 16 to 20.

    Channeled attacks that are simultaneously debuffs, such as Drain Life and Mind Flay, are now treated independently of the debuff limit; they do not count towards it, are not restricted by it, and do not knock off other debuffs if the limit is reached.

    [Note: This change is meant to make a small accommodation for alternative specs without enabling any massive shift in meta, while also providing some space for balance given the introduction of Deep Delve.]

  •  
    Tier 2.5 Acquisition
    The Brood of Nozdormu reputation required to acquire T2.5 set pieces has been increased. To obtain the boots and shoulders, players will need Friendly (up from Neutral). To obtain the helmet and legs, players will need Honoured (up from Friendly). And to obtain the chest, players will need Revered (up from Honoured).

    Festival Food
    Harvest Boar, Harvest Bread, Harvest Fish, Harvest Fruit, and Winter Veil Roast now restore 4% health per second for 25 seconds (up from 2%); Harvest Nectar and Winter Veil Eggnog now restore 4% mana per second for 25 seconds (up from 2%); and Bobbing Apple, Candy Bar, Candy Corn, Lollipop, and Winter Veil Cookie now restore 4% health and mana per second for 25 seconds (up from 2/3%; Bobbing Apple and Winter Veil Cookie still grant their well fed bonus) - bringing them all in line with Festival Dumplings. This normalises the food available at the four main festivals (Lunar Festival, Harvest Festival, Hallow's End, and Winter Veil). Graccu's Mince Meat Fruitcake, available at Winter Veil, remains slightly stronger due to it being a quest reward.
