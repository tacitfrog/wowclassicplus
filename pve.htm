Endgame Raids
  •  
    Deep Delve (Debuff)
    A new debuff called Deep Delve is applied to all players while inside any 40-man PVE raid dungeon (Molten Core, Blackwing Lair, Ahn'Qiraj, and Naxxramas). This debuff represents the gloom and despair of the deepest, darkest places in Azeroth and nullifies the effects of all buffs that are not applied by players or consumables. In other words, World Buffs no longer have any effect in 40-man raid dungeons.

    [Note: This change is meant to retain the over-the-top nature of World Buffs and to preserve them as a fun addition to the game, but to remove the toxicity associated with them and to restore the challenge to raid content.]

  •  
    Power Creep Mitigation
    To reduce power creep, many items from all 40-man dungeons after Molten Core, especially the weapons and trinkets from these dungeons, have had their item levels reduced and their stats re-balanced accordingly.

    Naxxramas gear has been nerfed the hardest, although it remains the highest item level gear in the game. This is because Naxxramas gear was originally balanced so that it would remain relevant up to level ~65 in TBC, it was not balanced for long-term usage in Vanilla: it is simply overpowered in Vanilla.

    Tiered set pieces have had their primary stats reduced to meet their new item levels. Tier 1 set pieces remain at item level 66. Tier 2/2.5/3 set items have had their item levels reduced from 76/78-88/86-92 to 72/74-80/78-84. These sets retain their powerful set bonuses and prestigious looks. (The Rank 12-13 PVP sets remain at item level 71-74.)

  •  
    Debuff Limit
    The maximum number of debuffs that can be on a target has been increased from 16 to 20.

    [Note: This change is meant to make a small accommodation for alternative specs without enabling any massive shift in meta, while also providing some space for balance given the introduction of Deep Delve.]

  •  
    Tier 2.5 Acquisition
    The Brood of Nozdormu reputation required to acquire T2.5 set pieces has been increased. To obtain the boots and shoulders, players will need Friendly (up from Neutral). To obtain the helmet and legs, players will need Honoured (up from Friendly). And to obtain the chest, players will need Revered (up from Honoured).

Tier 1.5
  •  
    Tier 1.5 Set
    A new 5-piece tier set for each class is available from Karazhan Tower and Lord Kazzak. These sets have item levels 66-70 and contain the following pieces: head, shoulders, chest, legs, and ring. These sets cater to specs that are usually not provisioned, such as rogue tank, shadow priest, and feral druid. Set bonuses:
     Hunter:
     3: Increases the damage done by your Kill Command by 5%.
     5: Increases the maximum focus of your pets by 20.
     Mage:
     3: All damage you take while channeling Evocation is reduced by 90%.
     5: Your Arcane Missiles critical hits invigorate you for 75-125 mana and add 40 spell damage to your next damaging spell ('Arcane Invigoration' effect, lasts 10 seconds).
     Rogue:
     3: When you dodge, you regain 2 energy (cannot occur more than once per second).
     5: When you suffer a critical hit, you gain 'Lurk in the Shadows' which reduces the crit chance of the next 3 attacks that hit you by 100% (lasts 10 seconds; melee, ranged, and spell crits; cannot occur more than once per minute).
     Shaman:
     3: Reduces the mana cost of your Lightning Bolt and Chain Lightning spells by 5%.
     5: Your offensive spell and melee critical hits cause one of your Lightning Shield orbs to strike the target.
     Warlock:
     3: Reduces the mana cost of your curses by 10%.
     5: Your Immolate ticks have a 3% chance to make your next Incinerate instant cast ('Firestorm' effect, lasts 10 seconds).
