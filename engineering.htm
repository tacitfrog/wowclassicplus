    Specialisation
    Engineering has always allowed players to craft items from both its specialisations, but in an awkward way (learning one, crafting the bind-on-pickup items, unlearning the whole profession, then re-learning). To avert this, players can now learn both specialisations - however, only one can be active at a time. The player is now free to complete both specialisation quests. When he completes the first, he will receive his first membership card with full duration. When he completes the other, the first membership card will be destroyed and replaced with the other with full duration. Full duration means that the membership card remains active for 14 days of logged-on time. It can be renewed for a fee at the appropriate NPC. If a membership card is not renewed, it will now self-delete when its timer depletes.

    If you visit the Gnomish Engineering NPC of your faction, one of two possible quests will be presented. If you already have an active membership card for Gnomish Engineering, it will cost 2 gold to renew it to full duration. Otherwise (if you have no card or a Goblin Engineering card), it will cost 50 gold to obtain a new Gnomish card with full duration (and your Goblin card will be destroyed). And vice versa: if you want to renew or switch to Goblin Engineering, you will need to visit the Goblin Engineering NPC of your faction. If you leave your membership card in your bank, you will not be able to renew or switch it - it needs to be in your inventory. Card renewal takes effect immediately; new cards will be mailed to you in one hour. A mail will be received in both cases with a chance to obtain specialisation-specific schematics.

    For the purpose of learning schematics, crafting items, and using items, you only count as being a Gnomish Engineer when your Gnomish card is active and in your inventory, and likewise for Goblin.

    The book Soothsaying for Dummies no longer offers any service for Engineers.

    Salvaging
    Engineers can now gather from the looted corpses of mechanical mobs, such as Harvest Golems. The list of possible items obtainable from each type of mob includes a number of appropriate engineering materials and junk items, and sometimes multiple items are obtained. Requires {(mob level - 3) * 5} Engineering skill.
