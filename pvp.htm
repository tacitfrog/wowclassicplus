Honour System
  •  
    Honour Carry-over and Rank Decay
    We define a player's Minimum Weekly Participation (MWP) to be {current rank * 100} honour. If the player earns honour during the week that meets or exceeds his MWP, he is considered to have been active in PvP during that week. All honour gained from kills, quests, and battlegrounds count towards MWP.

    To accommodate players who want to progress in PvP rank at a slower pace (and to prevent short-session honour gain from feeling futile): if the player meets his MWP but does not meet the minimum honour requirement for any progression at the end of the week, then his honour for that week (up to a cap of 100k) will carry over to the next week.

    To encourage participation, the player will need to meet his MWP each week or his rank will decay at the end of the week. The rate of decay is 10% of his current rank.

    The player's current rank is displayed on his nameplate. When inspecting a player, both his current and maximum ranks are listed. A player can continue to use weapons and equipment earned at a higher rank. However, players cannot use any battle standard or mount, or purchase anything new, that requires a rank that is higher than his current rank.

  •  
    Dishonourable Kills
    Dishonourable kills have been removed from the game. If a player kills a civilian, he will instead receive the Dishonourable debuff.

    All NPCs that are classified as civilians have had their health points doubled and their respawn time halved.

  •  
    Dishonourable (Debuff)
    The Dishonourable debuff is applied to players whenever they kill a civilian. It lasts for 1 hour and persists through death. While affected, enemy players will receive an extra 100% bonus honour whenever they kill the player and enemy faction guards in the open world (including towns and cities) will pursue the player 25% longer than usual if the player flees from them.

  •  
    Full Moon
    There is a new world PvP holiday every fourth weekend. See under Full Moon.
Battlegrounds
  •  
    Honour Quests
    To encourage players to participate in all three battlegrounds, the rewards for the Concerted Efforts and For Great Honor quests (both the initial and repeatable versions) have been improved.

    The honour given by these quests has been increased by 20% from 2388 to 2866.

    These quests now also award a choice of 1 Major Mana Potion or 1 Moist Towelette.

  •  
    Alterac Valley Holiday Bonus Honour
    The amount of holiday bonus honour given at the end of each Alterac Valley game during the Alterac Valley holiday has been halved.
  •  
    Battleground Emissaries
    For convenience (and to make cities feel alive), the patrolling emissaries for all battlegrounds will now walk around the capital cities all the time, not just during their holiday weekends. (The stationary emissaries and banners still only appear during their own holiday.)
  •  
    Preparation (Buff)
    To give a short grace period to casters for buffing, the Preparation buff (which reduces the mana cost of all spells to 0) is now cast on players for 3 seconds whenever they are respawned at a graveyard and whenever they join a game after it has already started.
  •  
    Portals
    To avoid grieving, portal spells cannot be cast in battlegrounds. This includes Mage portal spells and the Portal: Karazhan spell cast with Atiesh.
  •  
    Deserter (Debuff)
    The Deserter debuff still prevents the player from joining any battleground. However, it now works differently depending on how it was applied.

    If it is applied as a result of the player typing '/afk' or disconnecting, then it will last for 15 minutes.

    However, if it is applied as a result of actually being AFK in a battleground for 5 minutes or being reported by other players, then it lasts for 1 hour and all honour obtained by the player in that battleground is subtracted and his reputation with that PvP faction is reduced by 1000 (to a minimum of 1/3000 Neutral).

    In addition, if a player receives the 1-hour version 3 times in a single week, the third one will last for the entire rest of the week, preventing the player from joining any more battlegrounds that week.

    You can no longer report a player as AFK until 30 seconds after the game has started.

  •  
    Elemental Lords
    To prevent abuse, Ivus the Forest Lord and Lokholar the Ice Lord will reset when agro'ed by a player who is inside an entrance cave; they will not enter the cave and the elite guards at the entrance cave will not attack them.
  •  
    Bottled Alterac Spring Water
    The cost of Bottled Alterac Spring Water has increased to 60 silver (per 5), consistent with Blessed Sunfruit Juice.
