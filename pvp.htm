Battlegrounds
  •  
    Honour Quests
    To encourage players to participate in all three battlegrounds, the rewards for the Concerted Efforts and For Great Honor quests (both the initial and repeatable versions) have been improved.
    The honour given by these quests has been increased by 25% from 2388 to 2985.
    These quests now also award a choice of 1 Major Mana Potion or 1 Moist Towelette.
  •  
    Alterac Valley Holiday Bonus Honour
    The amount of holiday bonus honour given at the end of each Alterac Valley game during the Alterac Valley holiday has been halved.
  •  
    Battleground Emissaries
    For convenience (and to make cities feel alive), the patrolling emissaries for all battlegrounds will now walk around the capital cities all the time, not just during their associated holiday weekends. (The stationary emissaries and banners still only appear during their holiday.)
  •  
    Preparation (Buff)
    To give a short grace period to casters for buffing, the Preparation buff (which reduces the mana cost of all spells to 0) is now cast on players for 3 seconds whenever they join a battleground (after it has already started) and whenever they are respawned at a graveyard.
  •  
    Portals
    To avoid grieving, portal spells cannot be cast in battlegrounds. This includes Mage portal spells and the Portal: Karazhan spell cast with Atiesh.
Honour System
  •  
    Honour Carry-over
    To accommodate players who want to progress in PvP rank at a slower pace, and to prevent PvP feeling pointless when a player knows he will not be able to rank that week, if the player does not meet the minimum honour requirement for any progression (even a partial rank) at the end of the week, then his honour for that week (up to a cap of 100k) will carry over to the next week.
  •  
    Dishonourable Kills
    Dishonourable kills have been removed from the game. All NPCs that used to be categorised as civilians have had their health points increased and their respawn time decreased.
    A small number of crucial NPCs in starting zones are now unkillable to prevent the grieving of new players.
  •  
    Deserter (Debuff)
    When applied as a result of typing /afk or disconnecting from the game, the Deserter debuff lasts for 15 minutes.
    When applied as a result of being AFK for 5 minutes or being reported by other players, it lasts for 1 hour. In addition, in this case, all honour obtained by the player in that battleground is subtracted and his reputation with that PvP faction is reduced by 1000 (to a minimum of 1/3000 Neutral).
    If a player receives the Deserter debuff 3 times in a single week, they will be unable to join any more battlegrounds for the rest of the week.
    GMs now have the power to remove the ability for a player to report other players as AFK. This can be applied as a penalty if a player is found to be abusing the reporting system to grieve other players (such as by coordinated mass reporting of innocent players).
Full Moon
  •  
    Full Moon Holiday
    Every fourth weekend (whenever there isn't a battleground holiday), there is a new world PvP holiday from Friday to Monday called Full Moon (a.k.a. Phase 2 Weekend).
    During the holiday, all honourable kills made in the open world award an extra 25% bonus honour and the corpses of enemy players can be looted (if you received honour from the kill). Only one player can loot the corpse, just like in battlegrounds. Looting players yields some silver and a player head. Player heads are bind-on-pickup, based on the race of the corpse (e.g. Orc Head), unique (you can only carry one head, regardless of race), and start a hand-in quest called Enemy Head. If you loot a player corpse but already have a player head, the head will remain on the corpse for someone else to loot or until it despawns.
    New NPCs called Lunar Ritualists are permanently located in the six major cities. During the holiday, a Lunar Ritualist also appears in every non-neutral faction camp in all zones that are level 20 or over. During the holiday, all of these NPCs offer a repeatable quest called The Cleansing and end the (repeatable) Enemy Head quest.
  •  
    Full Moon Fervour (Buff)
    During the holiday, players get a buff called Full Moon Fervour whenever they kill an honour-awarding player in the open world. This increases their critical strike chance by 1%, lasts 60 minutes (or until death), and stacks up to five times. Even if you have five stacks, the duration is refreshed on each subsequent kill.
  •  
    Full Moon (Buff)
    During the holiday, all Night Elves (players and NPCs) get a persistent buff called Full Moon while outside that increases all damage dealt by 2% and dodge chance by 1%.
    [Note: This is a new racial trait for Night Elves.]
  •  
    The Cleansing
    Quest: kill 20 honour-awarding players of the opposing faction in the open world.
    Reward (level 20-29 when the quest is accepted): 25 silver, 82 honour, 20 Alliance/Horde reputation, and a choice of 2 Mana Potions or 2 Greater Healing Potions.
    Reward (level 30-39): 45 silver, 138 honour, 20 reputation, and a choice of 2 Greater Mana Potions or 1 Moist Towelette.
    Reward (level 40-49): 70 silver, 228 honour, 20 reputation, and a choice of 1 Superior Mana Potion or 1 Moist Towelette.
    Reward (level 50-60): 90 silver, 398 honour, 20 reputation, and a choice of 1 Major Mana Potion or 1 Moist Towelette.
  •  
    Enemy Head
    Quest: hand in this head to a Lunar Ritualist.
    Reward: 1 Fanatic's Cache.
    A Fanatic's Cache is a loot bag that shares a loot table with open-world Solid Chests, so it contains some silver, usually a green item, and various crafting materials and junk equipment. The level of the loot is based on the level of the player when the quest is handed in.
